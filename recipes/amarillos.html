Amarillos

Description

A dish tradtionally eater in Spanish Caribbean countries such as Puerto Rico and the Dominican Republic. It is made by frying ripe plantains and typically served to complement other foods such as grains or meat.

  • 3 ripe/yello plantain
    • <1i>2 cups oil
  • 3 tablespoons of salt
  1. Bring the oil to a boil under high heat
  2. While the oil is being heated, cut off the plantain skin with a knife and proceed to cut into slices
  3. After the oil is boiling, slowly and individually drop the slices into the oil
  4. After a few minutes or when the slices have turned golden on the bottom, flip them over
  5. After both sides have reached a golden hue, take the plantains out of the oil and turn off the heat
  6. Sprinkle the salt over the plantains and wait for it to cool down. Enjoy!
